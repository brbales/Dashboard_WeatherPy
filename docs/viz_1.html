Latitude vs. Max Temperature



The plot provides a clear visualization that temperature increases as you approach the equator at 0 degrees Latitude. Given the high number of samples and the reasonably even spread of those samples between -60 and 80 degrees of latitude we can infer that the observed patter is strong.


One point of particular interest from the plot is the minor outlier near the equator showing a temperature 15-20 degrees lower than the other sampled cities at similar latitude. Further exploration reveals that this point represents Tena, Ecuador. This city is in the high Amazon rainforest at the foothills of the Andes Mountains. It is at an elevation of about 2000ft above sea level providing an explanation for the variance that does not negate the conclusion.
