Latitude vs. Wind Speed



Based on the wind speed vs. latitude plot, there appears to be no correlation between latitude and wind speed. With the exception of a handful of cities, wind speed across all sampled latitudes remained between 0-30 mph. This fact lends itself to support the classification of hurricanes and tornadoes as extreme weather cases, given that wind speeds during these events greatly exceed what appears to be the normal range across much of the planet.
